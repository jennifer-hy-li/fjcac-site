Fujian-Canada Association

Constitution of the Fujian Chinese Association of Canada ( Draft)

Chapter 1. Title: The name of this association is the Fujian Chinese Association of Canada (formerly known as the Fujian Chinese Association of Canada) The English name is Fujian Community Association of Canada. Chapter II. Nature : The Association is a legally registered non-political, non-religious, non-profit social organization. Chapter III. Purpose and Mission: Article 1: Contact fellow villagers and serve them; help each other and cooperate to enhance relationships; establish and improve emergency response mechanisms to help fellow villagers in need; assist new immigrants to settle down and improve the image of fellow villagers; assist fellow villagers to adapt to and integrate into Canadian society ; safeguard the interests of members and the rights and interests of Chinese Canadians; promote exchanges, mutual assistance and cooperation among various Fujian community organizations in Canada, and strengthen cooperation with other Chinese groups and media. Article 2: Promote exchanges between Canada and China, especially economic, cultural, educational and other all-round exchanges between Canada and Fujian. Chapter 4 : Organizations and Institutions: The Federation of Fujian Chinese in Canada is composed of group members from Fujian communities in Canada and individual members from Fujian Chinese communities in Canada. It is led by a Standing Council and a co-leadership system consisting of an Executive Chairman, Executive Vice Chairman, Chairman, Supervisory Director, and Secretary General. Article 1: There shall be one executive chairman , elected from among the co-chairmen Article 2: There shall be three executive vice-chairmen , elected from among the co-chairmen Article 3: There shall be one Chairman , who shall be elected from among the co-chairmen. Article 4: There shall be one Chief Supervisor , who shall be elected from among the co-chairmen. Article 5 : There shall be one Secretary-General , who shall be elected from among the co-chairs. Article 6 : There shall be several co-chairmen, who shall be elected from among the executive directors. In order to meet the development needs of the Association, a number of entrepreneurs and public welfare enthusiasts from Fujian may be added as co-chairmen upon recommendation by more than three executive standing committee members and approval by more than two-thirds of the co-chairmen (with more than half of the co-chairmen present or authorized). Article 7 : There shall be a number of honorary chairmen, honorary advisors and vice chairmen For the development needs of the Association, upon recommendation by more than three executive standing committee members and approval by more than two-thirds of the co-chairs (more than half of the co-chairs present or authorized), a person with a good reputation or who has made special contributions to the Association may be appointed as Honorary Chairman, Honorary Advisor or Vice Chairman. Article 8: There shall be a number of executive directors to form the executive council. The executive directors are elected by the following methods: 1. The current president of any Fujian association registered in Canada; 2. Representatives recommended by more than 30 individual members; 3. Those who have made contributions to the Association among the previous executive directors and have been approved by half of the executive standing committee members; 4. Personnel who have made outstanding contributions to the association (volunteers, sponsors, buying tables, placing advertisements, etc.) Article 9: Appoint a number of directors Directors are recommended by the Secretariat and confirmed by the Standing Council after passing the review . Article 10: The Secretariat and the following institutions shall be established: As needed, departments such as Finance, Publicity, Business Relations, Employment, Legal, Senior Citizens, Women's Federation, Youth, Culture and Sports, Investment, and Overseas Study will be established. The co-chairs will serve as the heads of each department, and the deputy heads will be appointed by the heads and reported to the Standing Council for record. Chapter V. Elections and Change of Term: Article 1 : The term of office shall be renewed every two years. Election preparations shall be made one month in advance of each term. A preparatory election committee shall be formed from the previous Executive Standing Committee members to review the Standing Council units and convene a General Meeting of the Standing Council to elect the new co-chairs. Article 2 : A preparatory committee for the change of term shall be established upon nomination by more than half of the Executive Standing Committee members; a list of candidates for the new Executive Standing Committee shall be recommended from among the co-chairs upon approval by more than half of the Executive Standing Committee members. Candidates for the Executive Standing Committee shall meet the following requirements: 1. Mainly reside in Canada; 2. Have made significant contributions to the development of the Fujian Chinese community and the Association, and have high visibility and good reputation; 3. Ability to perform duties, actively serve, and possess strong leadership skills; 4. Provide campaign reports during elections ; 5. Cannot serve as the chairman of other associations with conflicts of interest at the same time; Article 3 : The Executive Chairman shall not be eligible for re-election upon the expiration of his or her term. During his or her term, the Executive Chairman shall fully represent the Association externally, perform his or her duties internally, implement his or her campaign report commitments, and carry out activities in accordance with the Association's purpose and mission. The Executive Chairman shall convene at least one Executive Council meeting annually and a Co-Chairs' meeting quarterly. Article 4 : When the Executive Chairman is unable to preside over the affairs of the meeting for some reason, the Executive Vice Chairman shall perform all duties on his behalf; when the Executive Chairman or the Executive Vice Chairman is unable to preside over the affairs of the meeting for some reason, the Secretary-General shall perform all duties on their behalf. Article 5 : Upon the expiration of the Executive Chairman’s term, he or she shall become Honorary Chairman with the approval of more than two-thirds of the current Co-Chairs’ Meeting (with more than half of the Co-Chairs present or authorized). Chapter 6. Member Management: Article 1 : Membership is voluntary Any Fujian community or individual in Canada who is willing to abide by the Association's charter and pay membership fees may apply to join the Association. The Association's membership consists of group members and individual members: 1. Group Membership Government-registered Fujian communities in Canada and Fujian entrepreneurs in Canada acting as organizations are eligible to apply for group membership. 2. Individual Membership Fujianese people living in Canada are eligible to apply for individual membership. Article 2 : Freedom to withdraw from membership Members may withdraw from the Association freely after providing written statement. Any Standing Director who fails to participate in the Association's activities within two years and fails to comply with the Standing Director's management regulations shall be deemed to have automatically withdrawn from the Association upon discussion and approval by the Co-Chairs' Meeting; Any Co-Chair who fails to participate in the Association's activities within two years and fails to comply with the Co-Chairs' management regulations shall be deemed to have automatically withdrawn from the Association upon discussion and approval by the Executive Standing Committee; Any Executive Standing Committee member who fails to participate in the Association's activities within two years and fails to perform corresponding obligations shall be deemed to have automatically withdrawn from the Association upon approval by more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairs' Meeting (with more than half of the Co-Chairs present or authorized). If any member at any level damages the reputation of the Association, violates the Association's Articles of Association, or breaks the law during his/her term of office, he/she shall withdraw from the Association on his/her own initiative or be removed by the consent of more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairs' Meeting (with more than half of the Co-Chairs present or authorized). Article 3: Member Rights 1. Qualified members have the right to vote and be elected 2. Participate in the activities of the association and enjoy the benefits of the association 3. Make criticisms and suggestions on the work of the Association 4. Free withdrawal Article 4: Member Obligations: 1. Comply with Canadian laws and regulations 2. Recognize and abide by the Articles of Association and voluntarily pay membership fees 3. Carefully implement the resolutions of the association and actively participate in the activities of the association 4. Maintain the legitimate rights and interests of the Association and promote its work 5. Executive Standing Committee members, co-chairmen and executive directors shall comply with relevant management regulations. Chapter VII. Financial Management: (The source and use of funds shall be subject to the principle of regular verification and timely disclosure. For details, please refer to the "Financial Revenue and Expenditure Management Measures". The following supplementary provisions will be incorporated into the "Financial Revenue and Expenditure Management Measures" after approval.) Article 1: Sources of Funding 1. Membership activity fees On the premise of not making a profit, we actively seek government and other sponsorships. If there is a gap, we will charge participants an activity fee at our discretion. 2. Sponsorship from entrepreneurs, members above the executive director level, and sponsorship and donations from relevant institutions, groups and enthusiastic individuals 3. Advertising and sponsorship fees for large-scale events and publications 4. Other income 5. Group membership fee : 100 yuan / year 6. Individual membership fee: 20 yuan / year, senior citizens and students: 5 yuan / year Article 2: Funding Management 1. Publish financial accounts at least twice a year ; 2. Publish financial accounts within one month after each major event ; 3. The Executive Chairman has the right to approve expenses within 2,000 Canadian dollars; if the Executive Chairman is unable to approve for some reason, the Executive Vice Chairman , the Chairman and the Secretary General shall sign and approve expenses within 1,000 Canadian dollars. 4. From the funds raised with the help of the Association , 15% will be set aside as an emergency fund to help those in sudden difficulties , except for funds for special designated purposes ; 5. In order to better promote the development of large-scale activities, relevant personnel can extract 10% of the business expenses from the advertising and sponsorship fees ; the preparatory group and staff can make reasonable expenses during the preparation period and reimburse the expenses, which will be the responsibility of the Secretary-General and reported to the finance department for verification afterwards. Chapter 8. General Association website and address: Launch and maintain the association's website; establish a relatively fixed clubhouse and telephone number; the association's headquarters is located in Toronto, Canada. IX. Supplementary Provisions The amendment of the charter shall be proposed by more than half of the executive standing committee members and shall be adopted by more than two-thirds of the co-chairs (more than half of the co-chairs are present or authorized) to form a new charter, which will be implemented after approval.
