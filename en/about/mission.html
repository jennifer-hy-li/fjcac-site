Fujian-Canada Association

Charter of the Fujian Community Association of Canada (Draft)

Chapter 1: Name

The name of the association is 为全加福建华人联合总会，（Formerly: 加拿大福建华联总会）.
English: Fujian Community Association of Canada.

Chapter 2: Nature

This association is a legally registered, non-political, non-religious, and non-profit organization.

Chapter 3: Purpose and Mission

Article 1

To connect and serve Fujianese in Canada; foster cooperation and mutual aid; enhance emotional bonds; establish emergency mechanisms to support members in need; help new immigrants settle and integrate; improve the image of Fujianese in Canada; safeguard the rights of members and Chinese Canadians; promote communication and collaboration between Fujian associations in Canada and with other Chinese community groups and media.

Article 2

To promote cultural, economic, and educational exchange between Canada and China, especially between Canada and Fujian province.

Chapter 4: Organization and Structure

The Fujian Community Association of Canada consists of group members from Fujian associations in Canada and individual members of Fujianese descent living in Canada. It operates under a collective leadership system led by the Executive Council, composed of the Executive Chairperson, Executive Vice-Chairpersons, President, Chief Supervisor, and Secretary-General.

Article 1

One Executive Chairperson shall be elected from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Article 2

Three Executive Vice-Chairpersons shall be elected from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Article 3

One President shall be elected from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Article 4

One Chief Supervisor shall be elected from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Article 5

One Secretary-General shall be elected from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Article 6

Several Co-Chairpersons shall be elected from among the Executive Council members.

For the development of the Association, upon the recommendation of at least three Executive Council members and with the approval of more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairpersons (with at least half in attendance or providing authorization), additional Fujianese entrepreneurs and individuals passionate about public service may be appointed as Co-Chairpersons.

Article 7

Several Honorary Chairpersons, Honorary Advisors, and Vice-Chairpersons shall be appointed.

For the development of the Association, upon the recommendation of at least three Executive Council members and with the approval of more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairpersons (with at least half in attendance or providing authorization), individuals with high prestige or special contributions to the Association may be appointed as Honorary Chairpersons, Honorary Advisors, or Vice-Chairpersons. One Lifetime Honorary Chairperson shall be appointed for each term.

Article 8

Several Executive Council members shall be appointed to form the Executive Council.

Executive Council members shall be selected by the following methods:

  • 1. Incumbent presidents of officially registered Fujian associations in Canada;
  • 2. Representatives recommended by at least 30 individual members;
  • 3. Members of the previous Executive Council who contributed significantly to the Association and were approved by at least half of the Executive Committee;
  • 4. Individuals who made outstanding contributions to the Association (e.g., volunteers, sponsors, table purchasers, advertisers).

Article 9

Several Council Members shall be appointed.

Council Members shall be recommended by the Secretariat and confirmed by the Executive Council after review.

Article 10

The Secretariat and the following departments shall be established:

Based on needs, departments may include: Finance, Publicity, Enterprise Liaison, Employment, Legal Affairs, Seniors, Women’s Affairs, Youth, Culture and Sports, Investment, and Overseas Study. Each department shall be headed by a Co-Chairperson, with Deputy Heads appointed by the Head and reported to the Executive Council for record.

Chapter 5: Elections and Term Renewals

Article 1

A new term shall be elected every two years. One month prior to the end of each term, preparations for the election must begin. The election committee shall be composed of members from the previous Executive Committee. This committee will review the list of Executive Council units and convene a General Assembly of the Executive Council to elect the new Co-Chairpersons.

Article 2

A re-election committee shall be formed upon nomination by more than half of the Executive Committee members and approved by the same majority. A list of candidates for the new Executive Committee shall be recommended from among the Co-Chairpersons.

Candidates for the Executive Committee must meet the following criteria:

  • 1. Reside primarily in Canada;
  • 2. Have made significant contributions to the Fujian Chinese community and the Association, with high reputation and credibility;
  • 3. Be able to fulfill their duties, serve actively, and possess strong leadership abilities;
  • 4. Submit a campaign report during the election;
  • 5. Must not simultaneously serve as the president of any other organization with conflicting interests;

Article 3

The Executive Chairperson may not be re-elected after their term ends. During the term, the Executive Chairperson shall fully represent the Association externally and fulfill duties internally, implementing campaign promises and carrying out activities aligned with the Association’s mission. The Executive Chairperson shall convene at least one Executive Council meeting per year and one Co-Chairpersons meeting each quarter.

Article 4

If the Executive Chairperson is unable to fulfill their duties, the Executive Vice-Chairperson shall assume full responsibilities. If both the Executive Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons are unavailable, the Secretary-General shall assume all duties.

Article 5

Upon completion of the term, the Executive Chairperson may become an Honorary Chairperson if approved by more than two-thirds of the current Co-Chairpersons (with at least half present or represented by proxy).

Chapter 6: Membership Management

Article 1

Voluntary Membership

Any Fujian association or individual in Canada who agrees to abide by the Association’s charter and pay the membership fee may apply to join. Membership consists of two types:

  • 1. Group Members: Registered Fujian associations in Canada and Fujian entrepreneurs may apply for group membership under their organization’s name.
  • 2. Individual Members: Individuals of Fujian origin residing in Canada are eligible to apply for individual membership.

Article 2

Freedom to Withdraw

Members may freely withdraw from the Association by submitting a written statement.

Executive Council members who do not participate in Association activities or follow Council rules for two years shall be deemed to have automatically withdrawn upon approval by the Co-Chairpersons' meeting. Co-Chairpersons who fail to do the same will be considered withdrawn upon approval by the Executive Committee. Executive Committee members who do not fulfill their duties for two years will be deemed withdrawn with the agreement of more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairpersons (with at least half present or represented).

Any member at any level who damages the reputation of the Association, violates the charter, or breaks the law should voluntarily resign or be removed by a two-thirds majority vote of the Co-Chairpersons (with at least half present or represented).

Article 3

Member Rights

  • 1. Eligible members have the right to vote and be elected
  • 2. Participate in Association activities and enjoy member benefits
  • 3. Provide feedback and suggestions about the Association’s work
  • 4. Freedom to withdraw at any time

Article 4

Member Responsibilities

  • 1. Comply with Canadian laws and regulations
  • 2. Agree with and follow the Association’s charter, and voluntarily pay dues
  • 3. Actively implement the Association’s decisions and participate in activities
  • 4. Safeguard the legitimate interests of the Association and support its work
  • 5. Executive Committee members, Co-Chairpersons, and Executive Council members must follow the corresponding management regulations

Chapter 7: Financial Management

(Funding sources and expenditures must be reviewed regularly and made public in a timely manner. For details, refer to the “Financial Management Policy.” The following supplementary clauses will be incorporated after approval.)

Article 1

Sources of Funds

  • 1. Member activity fees: Operated on a non-profit basis, with support from government and sponsors. Participation fees may be charged when necessary.
  • 2. Sponsorships and donations from entrepreneurs, Executive Council members, institutions, organizations, and generous individuals
  • 3. Advertisement and sponsorship fees from major events and publications
  • 4. Other income
  • 5. Group membership fee: $100 CAD per year
  • 6. Individual membership fee: $20 CAD per year; Seniors and students: $5 CAD per year

Article 2

Fund Management

  • 1. Financial reports shall be published at least twice per year
  • 2. Financial reports must be published within one month after each major event
  • 3. The Executive Chairperson has the authority to approve expenses under $2,000 CAD. If unavailable, two or more of the Executive Vice-Chairperson, Council Chair, or Secretary-General may approve expenses under $1,000 CAD
  • 4. Of the funds raised through the Association (excluding designated-use donations), 15% will be allocated as an emergency fund to assist members in unexpected hardship
  • 5. To better organize major events, 10% of sponsorship/ad revenue may be provided as business expenses to the personnel involved. The organizing team and staff may make reasonable expenditures during preparation, which shall be reimbursed by the Secretary-General and later verified by the finance department

Chapter 8: Association Website and Office

The Association shall establish and maintain an official website; a relatively fixed office location and phone line shall be set up. The office is located in Toronto, Canada.

Chapter 9: Supplementary Provisions

Amendments to this charter must be proposed by more than half of the Executive Committee members and approved by more than two-thirds of the Co-Chairpersons (with at least half present or represented). The new charter will take effect upon approval.
