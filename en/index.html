About Us

The Fujian Community Association of Canada (formerly the Fujian Chinese Association of Canada between 2012 and 2013) was founded in June 2003 as a non-political, non-religious Chinese community organization. Its mission is to unite Fujian Chinese in Canada, mobilizing the strength of individuals and groups across Canada to help fellow Fujianese, serve the community, and achieve common development. The Association elected a new Board of Directors after its fourth election. The new Board brings together new and established Fujianese immigrants and representatives from various sectors within Canada's Fujian community. With the strong support, assistance, and participation of fellow Fujianese, the Association actively organizes and participates in various social and community activities, promoting friendship, mutual assistance, and development among fellow Fujianese. It has gained increasing recognition from fellow Fujianese and the community, and continues to grow. Since its establishment, the Association has made significant contributions and achieved remarkable results in strengthening ties with fellow Fujianese around the world and their hometown, showcasing the unique characteristics of Fujianese, promoting multiculturalism in Canadian society, and promoting the prosperity of their hometown. This has helped Fujianese settle down and work in Canada. Fujianese Canadians are one of the largest groups within the Chinese Canadian community, currently numbering over 100,000. They are employed in various industries and are a significant force in the Chinese Canadian economy. Fujianese have a long tradition of residing abroad and engaging in entrepreneurship. Hard work, perseverance, and a willingness to win are hallmarks of the Fujianese spirit. This Fujianese spirit has flourished once again in Canada, creating a bright future and gaining recognition not only within the Chinese community but also in mainstream Canadian society. Large Asian supermarkets are commonplace in the Greater Toronto Area. Currently, nearly 100 Fujianese-run Asian supermarkets account for approximately two-thirds of the region's Asian supermarkets, employing over 5,000 people and benefiting both the Chinese community and the mainstream community. In the construction and renovation industry, many Fujianese-founded brands and businesses are also evident. According to incomplete statistics, there are over 100 Fujianese-owned construction and renovation businesses of all sizes. They are active in this sector, contributing to the development of Canada and Toronto. Fujianese have also successfully carved a niche in the restaurant industry. Currently, in the Greater Toronto Area, Fujianese-owned and operated nearly 200 restaurants, employing over 2,000 people. Fujianese have also excelled in financial services, food processing, education, healthcare, China-Canada trade, and vegetable farming. In recent years, with the arrival of investment immigrants, a group of Fujianese who have established successful businesses in mainland China have arrived in Canada. They bring with them capital, technology, management expertise, and entrepreneurial acumen. They are actively exploring Canada's socioeconomic environment and discovering new business opportunities in the new continent of North America. Many have already invested significant capital, acquired related assets and businesses, and are now successfully operating them. To express their gratitude for the trust of fellow Fujianese residents and to better serve their well-being in Canada, the fifth Board of Directors of the Fujian Chinese Association of Canada is composed of representatives from these diverse sectors. These include Executive Chairman Mr. Zheng Qifei , Chairman Ms. Wu Yimin , Executive Vice Chairman Wu Qiaoming, Ding Feng, Huang Jiaxiang , Chen Xu , Lin Maoling, Chen Guobo, Ms. Chen Junrong , Chief Supervisor Mr. Huang Jiaxiang, and Secretary-General Mr. Chen Xu. We believe that under the leadership of Executive Chairman Mr. Zheng Qifei and others , this council will, out of a sense of public welfare and do its utmost as a team, further improve mechanism construction, unite community forces more broadly, and make greater achievements in strengthening hometown ties, serving community development, and promoting Canada-China friendship.
